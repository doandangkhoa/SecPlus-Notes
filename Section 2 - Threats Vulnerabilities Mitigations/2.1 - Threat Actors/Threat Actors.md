## Key Attributes of Threat Actors
- **Location:** Can be **external** (outside the network) or **internal** (inside the organization).
- **Resources:** Ranges from limited (individuals) to extensive (governments, criminal syndicates).
- **Sophistication:** Varies from **unskilled** (using pre-made scripts without understanding) to **highly sophisticated** (building custom tools).
- **Motivation:** Includes financial gain, data theft, service disruption, political/philosophical reasons, or revenge.

## Types of Threat Actors

### 1. Nation State
- **Location:** External
- **Resources:** Extensive (government-backed).
- **Sophistication:** Very High (Advanced Persistent Threats or **APTs**).
- **Motivation:** National security, political reasons, data exfiltration, or disrupting critical infrastructure (e.g., military, utilities).
- **Example:** The **Stuxnet** worm (developed by the US and Israel) designed to destroy nuclear centrifuges.

### 2. Unskilled Attackers
- **Location:** Mostly External (sometimes Internal).
- **Resources:** Limited.
- **Sophistication:** Low (relies on existing scripts; lacks ability to troubleshoot or modify code).
- **Motivation:** Often the act of hacking itself, disruption, or simple data theft.

### 3. Hacktivists
- **Location:** Mostly External (can be Internal).
- **Resources:** Generally limited, though some fundraise.
- **Sophistication:** High (technically skilled).
- **Motivation:** Political or philosophical beliefs.
- **Actions:** Denial of Service (DoS), website defacement, or leaking private documents.

### 4. Insider Threats
- **Location:** Internal.
- **Resources:** High (already have access to internal systems).
- **Sophistication:** Medium.
- **Motivation:** Revenge, financial gain, or negligence.
- **Risk:** Difficult to detect; attackers know exactly where data is and how to bypass security controls. Requires strict vetting during hiring.

### 5. Organized Crime
- **Location:** External.
- **Resources:** High (corporate structure with dedicated roles for hacking, development, sales, and support).
- **Sophistication:** High.
- **Motivation:** **Profit** (e.g., ransomware, selling stolen data).

### 6. Shadow IT
- **Location:** Internal.
- **Resources:** Limited (departmental budgets/credit cards).
- **Sophistication:** Low (often lack IT/security background).
- **Risk:** Departments bypassing IT policies to use unauthorized cloud services or infrastructure without proper security, backups, or change control.

**Conclusion:** Understanding the specific type of threat actor allows organizations to tailor their security measures to prevent the most likely and dangerous attacks.
