## 1. Perimeter and Access Controls
*   **Barricades and Bollards:** Concrete barriers or bollards are used to channel foot traffic while preventing vehicles (cars, trucks) from entering sensitive areas. Brightly colored barricades also serve as visual warnings of high-security zones.
*   **Water Barriers:** Natural or man-made water features can force access through specific controlled points like bridges.
*   **Fences:** Used to clearly define boundaries.
    *   Can be **transparent** (allowing visibility) or **opaque** (hiding contents).
    *   High-security fences may be tall or topped with **razor wire** to prevent climbing.
*   **Access Control Vestibules (Mantraps):** A controlled room with two sets of doors designed to prevent tailgating.
    *   **Mechanisms:** Doors may be interlocked so that if one is open, the other remains locked.
    *   **Process:** Users badge in, wait for authentication (sometimes with security personnel), and then receive a second credential to enter the main facility.
    *   Common in large data centers.

## 2. Surveillance and Monitoring
*   **CCTV (Closed-Circuit Television):** Networked cameras that send video to a central storage point.
    *   **Intelligent Features:** Modern cameras include motion detection, object detection, facial recognition, and license plate reading.
*   **Security Guards:** Human personnel provide active monitoring and validation.
    *   **Two-Person Integrity:** Guards often work in pairs to ensure checks and balances, preventing a single individual from circumventing policies.
    *   **Role:** Validating employees and guests at entry points.

## 3. Identification and Authentication
*   **ID Badges:** Required for all personnel, typically worn visibly on lanyards or jackets.
    *   Contain photos, names, and details.
    *   Integrated with electronic locks to log entry times in a central database.

## 4. Environmental Controls and Sensors
*   **Lighting:** Well-lit areas deter unauthorized access by removing hiding spots.
    *   Crucial for **facial recognition** cameras to function effectively.
    *   Often used in parking lots with 24/7 camera monitoring.
*   **Infrared (IR) Technology:**
    *   Detects IR radiation to see in the dark without visible light.
    *   Used in cameras and **motion detectors** to alert on movement in limited areas.
*   **Pressure Sensors:** Detect changes in force when someone walks across a specific area, triggering an alarm.
*   **Microwave Detection:** Uses microwave signals to detect movement over **large areas**, offering more efficiency than IR for wide spaces.
*   **Ultrasonic Detection:** Sends sound waves and analyzes reflections to detect motion or provide collision detection (e.g., in loading zones).
